11-08-2021

We are officially registered with the Battle of Rockets Competition and we are working full speed ahead as our April 8th and 9th competition dates quickly approach. We are very excited to be participating in the Deployable Payload Event for the Battle of Rockets Competition! This is our first year as a team and we have a lot to update you on, so let’s jump in!

Build Team Update

For the past few months we have been planning and working on preliminary designs as well as testing out the rocket design. We are currently using “Open Rocket” and “SpaceCAD” to model our rocket’s performance. We are also using “Autodesk Inventor” and “Autodesk Fusion 360” to design the rocket structure.

Currently, we are modifying the CAD models to begin PLA 3D printing in the next few weeks for prototyping a model that we hope to launch. We are also planning on using SLA (Resin) 3D printed parts for our final prototypes.

Programming Team Update

We have finished designing code for the sensors in our payload which will utilize Arduino sensors. We also worked on the code for our ground station in Python with a GUI using matplotlib. Finally, we also programmed our team website from scratch, desgined by @kna27.

Now, we are waiting for the parts to come in. Then, we will be able to build, assemble, and upload the code onto the sensors.

Non-Tech Team Update

We have been busy behind the scenes making sure that the team can run fluently. We have made sure that the team is keeping to specific deadlines, and we are also working on securing funds. We have successfully ran a bake sale which brought in more than $350 in about 1 hour. However this will not be enough.

Currently, we are also reaching out to potential sponsors and coordinating logistics to run more bake sales in the coming months. We are also trying to work with our school to coordinate in person meetings and how to recruit freshmen. Coming soon will be our first-ever in person launch, which we will be coordinating. We are also working with NAR and Tripoli certified mentors to make sure we will be following all necessary precautions.
